## What is Tattoo Ink Ingredient Decoder?

The Tattoo Ink Ingredient Decoder is a free web tool that checks pigment names, Colour Index (CI) numbers, and CAS numbers against EU Regulation 2020/2081 (Annex XVII, Entry 75) and EU Regulation 2023/1545. It instantly identifies banned, restricted, and monitored substances in tattoo ink formulations.

The tool maintains a database of approximately 120 entries covering pigments, preservatives, heavy metals, aromatic amines, and other additives. It provides two search modes: a single ingredient lookup and a bulk decoder that extracts identifiers from full ingredient lists.

## Who Should Use This Tool

**Tattoo studio owners and artists** can verify their ink formulations comply with EU regulations before purchasing or using products on clients.

**Ink manufacturers and formulators** can screen new formulations and check existing products against current restrictions.

**Distributors and importers** can verify compliance documentation and identify non-compliant products in their inventory.

**Regulatory compliance officers** can perform quick checks on ingredient lists from suppliers and manufacturers.

**Permanent makeup professionals** can ensure their pigments meet the same regulatory standards as tattoo inks.

## How to Use

### Single Ingredient Search

1. Ensure the **Search ingredient** tab is active (default view).
2. Type a pigment name, CI number, or CAS number into the input field.
   - Examples: `Pigment Blue 15`, `CI 74160`, `147-14-8`, `Phthalo Blue`, `Carbon Black`
3. Press **Enter** or click the **Decode** button.
4. Review the result card showing:
   - Status icon and label (Banned, Restricted, Under Review, etc.)
   - Substance name and identifiers
   - Concentration limits (if applicable)
   - Regulatory notes
   - Alternative names
   - Links to ECHA registry and EU regulation

### Bulk Ingredient List Decoding

1. Click the **Decode ingredient list** tab.
2. Paste a full ingredient list into the text area.
   - Works with INCI lists, product labels, and Safety Data Sheet Section 3
   - Include CI numbers and/or CAS numbers for best results
3. Click **Decode All** to scan.
4. Review the results showing:
   - Statistics bar (counts by status category)
   - Alert banner (compliance status summary)
   - Sectioned results (Banned/SVHC, Restricted, Under Review, Not in database)

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### What is EU Regulation 2020/2081?

EU Regulation 2020/2081 amends Annex XVII of REACH (Entry 75) to restrict hazardous substances in tattoo inks and permanent makeup. It took effect on 4 January 2022 for new formulations and 4 January 2023 for existing stock.

### Does the tool check all tattoo ink ingredients?

The database covers approximately 120 entries including banned pigments, restricted additives, heavy metal limits, and aromatic amines. It focuses on substances explicitly listed in EU 2020/2081 and EU 2023/1545.

### What does "Not in database" mean?

It means the ingredient name, CI number, or CAS number was not found in our restricted substances database. The substance may be compliant, or it may not be a recognized pigment identifier.

### Can I use this for permanent makeup inks?

Yes. EU Regulation 2020/2081 applies to both tattoo inks and permanent makeup. The tool covers the same substances for both applications.

### How accurate are the results?

The tool uses the official EU regulations as its source. However, a negative result does not constitute regulatory clearance. Always verify with your supplier's EU compliance certificate and consult the ECHA restriction database.

### What do the concentration limits mean?

Restricted substances have maximum allowable concentrations in the finished ink product. For example, Carbon Black must contain ≤0.5 ppm total PAHs and ≤5 ppb benzo[a]pyrene. Heavy metals have limits in µg/g (parts per million).

### Does the tool check for nanoforms?

The database includes Titanium Dioxide as restricted, noting that ECHA is reviewing nanoforms separately. The tool does not specifically analyze particle size or nanomaterial status.

### How often is the database updated?

The database is maintained by Poli International and updated as new EU regulations take effect. The current version covers EU 2020/2081 and EU 2023/1545.

### Can I save or export results?

The tool displays results on screen. You can copy individual result card information or take screenshots for your records.

### What if I find an error in the database?

Contact Poli International through the website with the specific substance and correction details. The database is regularly reviewed and updated.
